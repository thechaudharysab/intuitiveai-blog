Intuitive Artificial Intelligence

Intuitive AI, a research project focused on developing artificial intelligence capable of intuitive decision-making, as opposed to purely logical reasoning. The core of this research involves a simulator, a 4x4 grid-based game inspired by Wumpus World, where users play and generate data that mirrors real-life psychological principles influencing intuition. Ultimately, the project aims to train AI to make "gut" decisions in uncertain situations by learning from human intuitive behavior recorded within the simulator.

How simulator mimic various elements of the real life

Free Will: Just as humans have free will in their decisions, users of the simulator can open any box at any given time, regardless of hints, or choose to follow their intuition to open any random box. This reflects the personal choice in real-life situations

Randomized Starting Point/First Hint: Each person has a unique starting point in life (e.g., being born into a rich vs. poor family, facing different challenges). Similarly, the simulator randomizes the grid every time a new game starts, creating a new environment. A random box is opened at the start, revealing a hint that is not always the best, mirroring how initial circumstances in life may or may not be favorable. This "random starting hint" is intended to make users face uncertain situations where intuitive decisions are needed.

Life as a 16-Box Maze: The simulator is described as the "psychology of life scaled down to a 16 boxes maze". Each grid or game can represent a "point of life" or a "life scenario"

Uncertainty and Unfamiliar Situations: The core idea is to reduce certainty as much as possible. Humans often take intuitive decisions in unfamiliar situations, and the simulator forces both humans and AI to make decisions based on intuition alone in uncertain environments. You have to focus on the "NOW" situation.

Fixed Rules (Laws of Life): Despite the randomness of the grid, the rules of the game remain constant, just like universal rules in life (e.g., birth, aging, death)

Individual Journey and Accountability: The simulator is designed with the individual in mind, reflecting the belief that each person is alone in their journey and answerable only to their own decisions. When playing, you are alone in a world that changes with your every decision

Failures Outweigh Successes: In life, people often experience more failures than successes, even if a single success might outweigh multiple failures. The simulator mimics this by having a high number of pits (4 pits in a 16-box grid, a 4:1 risk ratio), making the chance of failing higher than finding the goal.

Helping Hands/Guidance (Hints): Hints in the game (Breeze, Smell, Glitter, Nothing) are analogous to guidance or feedback from others in real life. While these hints provide knowledge and a sense of the future, the final decision remains with the player.

Secondary Goals / Optional Encouraged Behavior (EOB): The Gold element represents secondary goals in life. Pursuing them can be rewarding (extra points), but skipping them has no negative penalty, mimicking choices where one option might be more rewarding but the primary goal is still achieved. This adds a "greediness" factor, encouraging players to strive for more with an added risk.

The "NOW" Factor: The simulator forces players to focus on the immediate, present situation, as they open a random box with uncertain contents. This mimics the "NOW" factor in real-life intuitive decision-making, where individuals often make choices based on a gut feeling without knowing the exact outcome

Scoring System (Social Strata): The scoring (-1, 0, 1, 2) is built on psychological principles and can be seen as representing different life outcomes or social strata:
▪ 1 point: Represents the majority (80%) who are "middle class" and strive for acceptable outcomes
▪ 2 points: Achieved by those who take calculated risks and go out of their way to succeed, representing "successful" individuals
▪ 0 points: Represents those who were doing well but died early, leaving a legacy that prevents total loss. They don't win but doesn't lose everything.
▪ -1 point: The lowest possible score, representing total failure, whether from calculated risks or random, careless decisions

Life Situations as Games: Each game played on the Intuitive AI simulator reflects one scenario of life where a person is taking intuition-based decisions within certain rules

--------------------------------
The simulator encourages both calculated and intuitive decisions. It defines intuition as a "gut feeling" or "hunch", which is not always logical. The design aims to replicate real-life decision-making where a choice is made based on feeling rather than pure logic ("I just felt like opening this box"). The overall goal is to enable AI to take "non-calculated risks" when logic is insufficient.

the simulator aims to capture many aspects of life, but it also has deliberate simplifications, such as the truthfulness of hints, which means it does not fully capture the deceptive elements present in real-life advice. But the goal of this simulator is to create an environment that capture human decision making process where somethings we're sure about and somethings we use intuition to solve.
